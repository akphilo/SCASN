Who We Are

The Sickle Cell Support and Awareness Network (SCASN) is an independent non-governmental, non-partisan, and not-for-profit organization. Our mission is to serve as a dedicated center committed to mitigating the increasing prevalence and burden of Sickle Cell Disease (SCD) and other inherited disorders. We strive to provide a supportive and educated community, bringing hope and resources to those affected by SCD.

High SCD Prevalence

The prevalence rate of the sickle cell trait is about 20%, much higher than the national average of 13.3%.

Lack of Awareness

Many people do not know their sickle cell status, leading to high rates of children being born with SCD.

Testing and Diagnosis

There is a need for increased testing and diagnosis. Many find out they have SCD only during crisis situations, rather than through proactive screening.

Times of pain and pleasure truly must be endured; it is easy for those who are blinded to be held to the truth, and distinction becomes possible.
